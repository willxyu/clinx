## Abdominal, Hepatomegaly %%
Causes of hepatomegaly?
- Metastases
- Hepatocellular carcinoma
- Alcoholic liver disease with fatty infiltration
- Right heart failure
- Myeloproliferative disease
- Haemochromatosis
- CML, lymphoma
- NAFLD, NASH
- Hepatitis (drugs, infection)
- Infiltrative (amyloid, sarcoid)
- Cirrhosis
- Granulomatous disease
- Biliary obstruction
- Hydatid disease
- HIV, ischaemia

## Abdominal, Splenomegaly %%
Causes of splenomegaly?
- CML
- Myelofibrosis
- Hairy cell lymphoma
- Kala-azar
- Malaria
- Portal hypertension
- Lymphoma
- Leukemia
- Thalassaemia
- Gaucher's disease
- PRV, ET
- Anaemias: megaloblastic/hemolytic
- Infective: CMV, EBV, IE
- Infiltrative: Sarcoid, amyloid
- Connective: SLE, RA, PAN

## Abdominal, Hepatosplenomegaly %%
Causes of hepatosplenomegaly?
- Chronic liver disease with portal hypertension
- Haematological: Myeloproliferative disease, lymphoma, leukemia, pernicious anaemia, sickle cell
- Infection: Hepatitis, glandular fever, CMV
- Infiltrative: Sarcoidosis, amyloidosis
- Connective: SLE
- Acromegaly
- Thyrotoxicosis

## Abdominal, Pulsatile Liver %%
Causes of pulsatile liver?
- Tricuspid regurgitation
- Hepatocellular carcinoma
- Arteriovenous malformations

## Abdominal, Hepatic %%
Causes of tender liver?
- Acute hepatitis
- Rapid engorgement (right heart failure, Budd-Chiari)
- Hepatocellular carcinoma

## Abdominal, Spleen %%
Distinguishing features of a spleen?
- No palpable upper border
- Notch
- Inferomedial movement
- Not ballotable
- Not resonant
- Friction rub

## Abdominal, Renal Mass %%
Causes of a renal mass?
- Hydro/pyonephrosis
- Hypernephroma (RCC)
- Renal vein thrombosis, acute
- Polycystic kidneys (bilateral more often)
- Amyloid, lymphoma (bilateral)
- Acromegaly (bilateral)
- Solitary kidney

## Abdominal, Polycystic Kidney %%
What else should you check when you find polycystic kidneys?
- Blood pressure
- Anaemia/polycythaemia
- Hepatic cysts
- Splenic cysts
- Haematuria

## Abdominal, Jaundice %%
Differentials for jaundice?
- Increased turnover (haemolysis)
- Ineffective erythropoeisis (sickle cell)
- Hepatic congestion (cardiac failure, rifampicin)
- Impaired conjugation (Gilbert's syndrome)
- Hepatocellular dysfunction (viral hepatitides, alcoholic cirrhosis, haemachromatosis, Wilson's disease, alpha-1)
- Impaired excretion (alcoholic liver disease, primary biliary cirrhosis, ascending cholangitis, pancreatic obstruction)

## Abdominal, Haemachromatosis %%
You find isolated hepatomegaly with slate-grey skin pigmentation. What are concurrent signs of haemachromatosis?
- Scant axillary hair
- Abdominal lipodystrophy
- Displaced apex beat from cardiomyopathy
- Second & third metacarpophalangeal joint arthropathy
- Testicular atrophy

## Abdominal, Haemachromatosis %%
What tests will confirm a clinical suspicion of haemachromatosis?
- Initially, transferrin saturation more than 40 percent
- Serum ferritin of more than 1,000 micrograms per litre
- HFE genotyping looking for C282Y or H63D
- MRI liver
- Liver biopsy with Perl staining

## Abdominal, Haemachromatosis %%
Aside from diagnostic tests for haemachromatosis, what are tests would be useful in your management?
- Any diagnostic battery for diabetes mellitus
- 12-lead ECG & echocardiogram for cardiomyopathy
- Assessment of gonadotrophin secretion & other pituitary function

## Abdominal, Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis %%
What is the prevalence of IBD in patients with PSC?
- 90% (UC mainly)
- only 5% in the opposite (IBD patients with PSC)

## Abdominal, Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis %%
What are the risks of PSC?
- Cirrhosis
- Cholangiocarcinoma
- Gallbladder cancer
- Colorectal carcinoma
